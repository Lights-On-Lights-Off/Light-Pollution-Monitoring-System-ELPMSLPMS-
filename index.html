Light Pollution & This System

Understanding the environmental impact of artificial light at Northern Bukidnon State College

💡

What is Light Pollution?

Light pollution is the unwanted or misdirected artificial light that brightens areas where it isn't needed, disturbing the natural darkness. It can affect wildlife, disrupt human sleep and health, and even hide the beauty of the night sky.

🌃

Sky Glow

The night sky brightens over populated areas, hiding stars and diminishing our connection to the cosmos. Urban and campus lighting contributes significantly to this phenomenon.

🔦

Light Trespass

Light spilling into spaces where it doesn't belong — from streetlamps entering bedroom windows to floodlights crossing property boundaries — a common but solvable problem.

⚡

Over-Illumination

Using more light than necessary wastes energy, increases operating costs, and adds unnecessary brightness to the environment. Smart sensing helps identify these inefficiencies.

🗺️

About This System

The NBSC Light Pollution Monitoring System measures and maps light pollution across campus in real time, helping the community make smarter energy choices and create a greener environment.

PURPOSE

Why This System Exists

Our goals, mission, and the community impact we aim to create at NBSC

🎯

Our Mission

To provide real-time, data-driven insight into campus light pollution so administrators, students, and faculty can make informed decisions that reduce environmental impact and improve campus life.

🌿

Environmental Responsibility

Excessive lighting harms nocturnal wildlife, disrupts ecosystems, and wastes energy. By monitoring and reducing unnecessary light output, NBSC moves toward a more sustainable future.

📊

Data-Driven Decisions

Rather than guessing where lighting improvements are needed, this system provides precise lux readings per building — empowering campus management with actionable, evidence-based data.

💰

Energy Savings

Identifying over-illuminated areas leads directly to reduced electricity consumption. Smarter lighting schedules and targeted adjustments can yield significant cost savings for the institution.

🎓

Academic Value

This system serves as a living laboratory for students in environmental science, IT, and engineering — offering hands-on exposure to IoT sensing, data visualization, and environmental monitoring.

🌟

Community Awareness

By making light pollution data visible and accessible, we raise awareness among the broader NBSC community — inspiring a culture of environmental consciousness that extends beyond the campus.
